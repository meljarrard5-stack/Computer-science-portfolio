## Reflection

### Interpreting User Needs
I interpret user needs by listening carefully to stakeholders, asking clarifying questions, and identifying the problem they want solved. I then translate those needs into program requirements and priorities. User stories help by describing features from the user's perspective and keeping development focused on delivering value.

### My Development Approach
I approach development by breaking projects into manageable tasks, prioritizing work, and improving through feedback. Agile practices such as sprint planning, backlog management, daily communication, sprint reviews, and retrospectives help teams remain organized and adaptable. In future projects, I hope to continue using iterative development and continuous improvement.

### Being a Good Team Member
Being a good team member means communicating clearly, meeting commitments, respecting others’ ideas, and supporting shared goals. Successful software development depends on collaboration, accountability, and a willingness to help the team succeed.
